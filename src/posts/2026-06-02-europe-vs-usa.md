Over the last 25 years, U.S. productivity growth outpaced Europe's by ~1% annually. Critics (and the Mario Draghi report) warn Europe is in terminal decline — due to over-regulation, fragmented capital markets, and a total failure to scale tech giants. Yet European living standards (purchasing power) have held steady at roughly 80% of the U.S. level. Why?

**Free Rider Effect — Krugman**
- U.S. dominates high-tech production, but competition causes lower global tech prices
- Benefits of U.S. innovation flow outward — Europe benefits from U.S. productivity

**Krugman's Statistical Illusion — Krugman's Critics**
- Krugman's measures rely on current Purchasing Power Parity (PPP), repricing the economy yearly and masking actual shrinkage
- Actual U.S. lead is massive and widening

**Welfare vs. Power**
- Krugman: Health outcomes, vacation time, safety nets, purchasing power — Europe is stable
- Critics: Innovation ownership matters — Europe loses profits and long-term geopolitical leverage
