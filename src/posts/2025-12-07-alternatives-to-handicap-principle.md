Source: [Zollman et al. (PDF)](https://www.kevinzollman.com/uploads/5/0/3/6/50361245/alternativestohandicap.pdf)

In biology, honest communication is stable also in situations where interest of sender and receiver conflict, e.g.
- Mate choice
- Parent-offspring interactions
- Prey-predator signaling
- Competitor signaling

Why?

**Solution 1 - Handicap Principle:** Signals are costly (e.g. peacock tail)
- Some empirical problems;
- Problem of existence of pooling equilibria, in which e.g. the parent always offers resources - why should signalling evolve?

**Solution 2 - Partial Pooling:** Signals combine status information (e.g. same signal for hungry + desperate)
- Dishonest signalling is less beneficial because the signal is pooled with group norms;
- Costs for signalling decrease.

**Solution 3 - Hybrid Equilibria:** Signals are sampled from distributions on individuals' status
- No static stable equilibria, but dynamic;
- Some models show cycling around static equilibria (e.g. total pooling);
- Potentially likely to evolve biologically.

**Solution 4 - Reputation:** Signalling is a repeated game
- Signal receivers may calculate average frequencies and compare signals. In the long term, they learn how honest individuals are.

**Solution 5 - Correlated Interactions:** Signallers interact often, so their interests are aligned
- Altruism: A type of group selection?

**Solution 6 - Signs vs. Signals:** Some signals cannot (easily) be faked
- Undefined boundary between signals and signs;
- Some signs can only be faked with an error.

**Solution 7 - Miscellaneous**
- Runaway processes in mating strategies: Ever more extreme ornaments and ever more extreme preferences;
- (In-)Direct observability: E.g. hunger as a consequence of observable amount of food, or thinness;
- Sensory exploitation: Manipulation of pre-existing preferences (e.g. red-colour mate is preferred because of red-colour foraging preferences).
