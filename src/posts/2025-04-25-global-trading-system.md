date: 2025-04-25

Source: [Foreign Affairs](https://www.foreignaffairs.com/world/global-trading-system-was-already-broken)

**Problem**
- Businesses (and countries) suppress wages to be competitive. This benefits them in the short-term but reduces global demand (Kalecki's Paradox of Costs).
- Global trade surpluses are ultimately caused by wage suppression to boost manufacturing and exports (and thereby reduce domestic demand); countries like the US run trade deficits, which they offset by running budgetary deficits.

**Solution: Keynes' 1944 Plan (at Bretton Woods)**

A customs union in which trade stays multilaterally balanced, and which imposes trade barriers on non-members who run persistent surpluses.
