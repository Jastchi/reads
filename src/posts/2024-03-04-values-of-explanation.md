Four values for preferring different types of explanation:
1. Descriptiveness - are many aspects of the topic consequences of the explanation?
2. Domain knowledge - is the explanation consistent with the established knowledge?
3. Co-explanatory - does the explanation link up many aspects of the topic?
4. Aesthetics/Simplicity - is the explanation simple (s. Kolmogorov complexity)?

How do different scientific disciplines weigh these values?

**Conspiracy theories as overweighing**

Conspiracy 1: The Oklahoma City Bomber was caught speeding in a car without license plates directly after the attack. He was smart enough to carry out the attack, how could he have been so stupid to get caught this easily? The mainstream explanation does not explain this well enough (low descriptive value). Instead, the government is behind it, the attacker just a scapegoat (high descriptive value, low domain knowledge value).

Conspiracy 2: The Sovereign Citizen Movement believes that the government has created a duplicate citizen for each real citizen, and taxes that duplicate. When writing the tax authority, it addresses the citizen in capital letters, which is the name of the duplicate citizen. This explains why your name on your driver's license is in capital letters by the way (high co-explanatory value, low domain knowledge).
