The following revolutions are the top 4 revolutions with regards to the role in shaping the modern liberal order:

**The Dutch Republic (17th century)**
- Tolerance, free trade, decentralised politics;
- Thanks to geographic pressures like flood control.

**The Glorious Revolution (1688, England)**
- Religious tolerance, Monarchy became parliamentary.

**The French Revolution (1789)**
- Radical, top-down approach, leading to terror, instability;
- Led to classical modern conservatism (e.g. Edmund Burke).

**The Industrial Revolution (late 18th century, Britain)**
- Bottom-up, decentralised;
- Redefined the source of power, through productivity and innovation.

The 21st century is shaped by the following top 4 revolutions:

**Globalisation**
- Expanded opportunity;
- Fueled displacement, inequality, political backlash.

**Technology & Digitalisation**
- Transformed economies;
- Spreads misinformation and social fragmentation.

**Identity Politics**
- Shift from Left-Right economic debates to Open-Closed cultural debates;
- Fragments societies into tribes - traditional identities are replaced by political identities, leading to polarisation.

**Geopolitics**
- From bi- or tripolar, via potentially unipolar, to multipolar world order;
- Historically, the threat of challenges to hegemony lead to great wars;
- After decades of international political cooperation, return of Realpolitik.

**Takeaways:**
- Decentralised, gradual change succeeds better then top-down, ideological change;
- Institutions and local cohesion matter more than ideology;
- Today's crises echo past revolutions, but are more global, fast, and complex. They admit no one template solution.
