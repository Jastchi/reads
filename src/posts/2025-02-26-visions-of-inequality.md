**François Quesnay ~1750**
- First economist - Tableau économique
- Three classes proprietary (landowners, should be taxed), productive (farmers), sterile (merchants, useless)

**Adam Smith ~1770**
- Free market, but need for regulations and public institutions to prevent e.g. monopolies and rent-seeking
- Critique of laws against labour unions without any against "combinations of masters"

**David Ricardo ~1800**
- Definition of rents, increasing in particular for landowners due to land scarcity
- For free trade and industry specialisation (comparative advantage)
- Wages naturally tend toward subsistence

**Karl Marx ~1850**
- Worker exploitation by capitalists
- Falling rate of profit with increasing capital lead to crises

Marx' theory may lead to four evolutions of income distribution:

1. Increasing real wages, declining profit rate - Western Europe?
2. Increasing real wages, increasing capital concentration - USA?
3. "Immiseration of labour", decline of profit rate - some Latin American societies?
4. "Immiseration of labour", increasing capital concentration - South Africa?

**Vilfredo Pareto ~1900**
- Income inequality is permanent, even when removing certain classes (Pareto distribution)

**Simon Kuznets ~1940**
- Kuznets curve: Shifts from agriculture to industry initially increase inequality before decreasing again
- Multiple cycles with technological gains, wars, globalisation?

**The Long Eclipse of Inequality Studies ~1950-2000**
- Ideological suppression in capitalist and socialist nations
- Emphasis on aggregate values like GDP
- New approaches "failed to recognize that the marginal productivity of capital is a technical matter, and that capital yields an income to its owner only when there is a 'social contract' or economic system in place that enables owners of tools (including capital) to collect the products of the tools they own"
