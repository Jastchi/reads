**Overview**
- Chokepoints include financial markets, maritime trade routes and insurances, advanced technology exports;
- The USA have outsized leverage;
- Need for more systematic doctrine of economic statecraft.

**Case Studies**

*Iran*
- Isolation from financial networks by sanctioning of third-party providers and banning U-turns;
- Use of dollar and banking chokepoints.

*Russia*
- Decoupling from capital markets by SWIFT cutoff and freeze of central bank reserves;
- Oil price cap, enforced by denying Western insurance on Russian oil tankers;
- Use of financial, capital, technological chokepoints.

*China*
- Export control of advanced technologies, including semiconductors;
- Sanctioning of Chinese companies, e.g. Huawei (who lost global dominance).

**Problems**
- International cooperation and collateral damage;
- Incentivisation of alternatives - economic blocs instead of centrality of USA;
- Use as deterrent is paradoxical: Preparations in case of knowledge of concrete potential sanctions, underestimation otherwise.
