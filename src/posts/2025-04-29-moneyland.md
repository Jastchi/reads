**The Gold Standard**
- After Bretton Woods (1944), currencies were pegged to gold. Hence, countries needed to prevent currency speculation (e.g. mass selling of a currency, which the country would need to absorb to keep exchange rates stable, depleting their reserves - see Black Wednesday) by imposing capital controls - no significant offshore wealth;
- Eurodollars, U.S. dollars held in banks outside of the U.S., emerged as a parallel dollar system;
- The first Eurobonds (bonds issued in a currency not native to the country where they are issued) were denominated in Eurodollars; they were essentially loans to Autostrade, paying dividends, with ownership tied to the possession of the physical bond certificates; they formed a new commodity, tradable outside of capital controls;
- The gold standard was dismantled in 1971, creating Moneyland, a system in which capital can escape state laws through legal arbitrage.

**Features of Moneyland after the Gold Standard**
- Offshore jurisdictions (low or zero taxes, no ownership declaration requirements, secrecy laws) - Nevis, Jersey, Cayman Islands, Cyprus;
- Citizenship by investment (for tax, extradition, sanction avoidance) - St. Kitts & Nevis, Malta, Cyprus, Austria (very small number);
- Diplomatic immunity (for immunity from prosecution, protection from divorce settlements) - St. Kitts & Nevis.

**Future of Moneyland**
- After whistleblowers (e.g. Bradley Birkenfeld on UBS and Credit Suisse), the U.S. forced foreign banks to report U.S. citizens' accounts; many countries (not the U.S.) take part in automatic exchange of financial information;
- U.S. states like Nevada implement dynasty trusts (distribution after 300+ years) - capital is transferred to trustees, who manage it according to the settlor's will, until it is distributed to the beneficiaries; inheritance taxes are only payable upon distribution to the beneficiaries;
- Nevada trusts have no requirements to list owners; assets of foreigners are not reported to their home countries; there are legal loopholes for tax evasion for trusts with foreign trustees and domestic beneficiaries - a new tax haven in the U.S.
